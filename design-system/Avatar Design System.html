Design System

A digital twin that
doesn't look like a chatbot.

Avatar lets visitors talk to a digital twin of the site owner — and lets the owner step into any conversation live. This system gives that experience a sharp, editorial, dark-first identity: blue for the visitor, cyan for the twin, and yellow reserved for the human-in-the-loop.

Visitor · blue Avatar · cyan Human · yellow

Design language

Dark-first and navy-tinted. Editorial serif paired with a crisp grotesque and a mono technical layer. One accent per role, no decoration for its own sake.

  • Not a generic chatbot. Asymmetric bubbles, an editorial serif voice, and a real composer — refined, not the default bot shell.
  • Color carries meaning. Blue = visitor, cyan = the twin, yellow = the human stepping in. Purple is locked to primary actions only.
  • Technical honesty. Tool use, the Qn shortcut, and conversation ids are surfaced in mono — the machinery is part of the aesthetic.

Do

  • Use solid fills and hairline borders
  • Keep yellow for the human only
  • Let the serif do the talking at display sizes
  • Surface tool use in small mono

Don't

  • No gradients in chrome, no purple wash
  • No left-edge accent bars on content
  • No emoji, anywhere
  • No drop-shadow glow as decoration

Motion & texture

Motion is quick and purposeful. Three durations (120 / 220 / 420ms), an ease-out for entrances and a standard ease for state. Press states nudge 1px, never bounce. The canvas always carries a faint grid — swappable to crosses or rings via --grid-mark.

Build notes

Vanilla TypeScript + Vite. Copy tokens.css, components.css, icons.svg and assets/ into the frontend; load order is tokens → components → page CSS. Default theme is dark, persisted in localStorage['avatar-theme']. Derive every color from tokens — never invent one.
